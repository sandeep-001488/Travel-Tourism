    • Diu, Daman and Diu:

    Diu is a small island town known for its tranquil beaches, Portuguese architecture, and a laid-back atmosphere. The Diu Fort, St. Paul's Church, and the pristine Nagoa Beach are some of the attractions. Diu is less crowded compared to some other beach destinations in India.

    Bhandardara, Maharashtra:

    Bhandardara is a picturesque hill station and a hidden gem in the Sahyadri Range of Maharashtra. It is known for its serene lakes, lush greenery, and the famous Arthur Lake. The Randha Falls and the historical Ratangad Fort are also nearby.

    Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park, Gujarat:

    This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a lesser-known treasure with historical and archaeological significance. It features well-preserved Islamic and Hindu architecture, including mosques, tombs, and forts. The Champaner-Pavagadh Park is a testament to Gujarat's rich history and heritage.

    Dang District, Gujarat:

    The Dang District in Gujarat is a tribal region known for its lush forests, waterfalls, and vibrant indigenous culture. Saputara, a hill station in the Dang District, offers scenic views, boating on Lake Saputara, and tribal craft markets.

    Saputara, Gujarat:

    Saputara is one of the few hill stations in Gujarat, nestled in the Western Ghats. It's known for its cool climate, lush gardens, and panoramic views from the Sunset Point. The Artist Village showcases local tribal art and culture.
