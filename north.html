• NORTHERN BLOCK

    • Spiti Valley, Himachal Pradesh:

    Located in the cold desert region of the Himalayas, Spiti Valley is often referred to as "Little Tibet" due to its Tibetan culture and landscapes. The valley is home to several ancient monasteries, including Key Monastery, one of the largest in the region. Spiti Valley remains cut off from the rest of the world during the winter months, making it a secluded and pristine destination for adventurous travelers.

    Gurez Valley, Jammu and Kashmir:

    Gurez Valley is a remote and breathtakingly beautiful region in the northernmost part of Jammu and Kashmir. The valley is surrounded by snow-capped peaks and is crisscrossed by the Kishanganga River. Due to its proximity to the Line of Control (LOC), Gurez Valley has been off-limits to foreign tourists for a long time, but domestic tourists are gradually discovering its charm.

    Champawat, Uttarakhand:

    Champawat is a historic town in Uttarakhand that was once the capital of the Chand dynasty. The town is known for its ancient temples, including the Baleshwar Temple, which dates back to the 10th century and features intricate stone carvings. Champawat is surrounded by lush forests and offers opportunities for trekking and wildlife exploration.

    Nubra Valley, Ladakh:

    Nubra Valley is a high-altitude desert in Ladakh, known for its dramatic landscapes, including sand dunes and double-humped Bactrian camels. The valley is accessible via the Khardung La Pass, one of the highest motorable passes in the world. Travelers can stay in traditional Ladakhi villages and monasteries while exploring this unique region.

    Majuli Island, Assam:

    Majuli is the world's largest river island, located in the Brahmaputra River in Assam. The island is known for its vibrant culture, including traditional Assamese dance and music. It is also an ecologically sensitive area and has been recognized as a biodiversity hotspot.
